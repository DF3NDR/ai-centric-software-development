# The AI-Centric Software Development Playbook

A practitioner's guide to building software in the age of AI — designed for small, highly capable teams that leverage AI tools to deliver what large organizations once required.

## 📖 About This Repository

This repository contains a comprehensive series of parts that establish a methodology for modern software development in which small teams (1–5 people) orchestrate AI tools to perform specialist-level work across every discipline. These are **living documents** — continuously refined and updated as the field evolves and new practices emerge.

**Timeline Thesis:** At some point near the end of 2026 to late 2027 teams on the leading edge will transition to something like this approach.

**Core Philosophy:** This is not about replacing engineers. It's about multiplying the leverage of skilled practitioners by giving them orchestration tools — prompts, skills, instruction sets, MCP servers, custom AIs — that compress what large teams once did into small, highly capable units. The fundamentals of computer science, architecture, and design thinking remain essential. What changes is the *interface* through which that knowledge is applied.

## 🎯 What You'll Learn

This playbook covers the complete software development lifecycle through an AI-centric lens:

- **Specification Driven Development (SDD)** — An iterative, specification-first methodology that forms the engine of the framework
- **Core Principles** — Six guiding principles (Security, Maintainability, Economics, Operations, Scoring & Metrics, Correctness Verification) that travel with you through every phase
- **Tool Sets** — Curated combinations of prompts, skills, MCP servers, instructions, and resources assembled for specific purposes
- **Practical Application** — How to apply these concepts across every phase from information gathering through deployment and evolution

## 🔑 Key Concepts

### Specification Driven Development (SDD)

The iterative, specification-first workflow that powers the AI-centric approach:

1. **Interview prompts** gather needs and context
2. A **plan document** is produced and broken into sections or epics
3. Each section is specified through a detailed **Project Requirement Document (PRD)**
4. The PRD drives a well-defined **task list**
5. **Implementation prompts** produce results

This SDD cycle runs continuously across all phases, with Core Principles evaluated at every step.

### The Toolkit Philosophy

**Principles First, Then Tools** — Every building block in your toolkit (prompts, skills, MCP servers) should be evaluated against the six Core Principles. If a tool accelerates development but undermines a principle, it doesn't belong.

**Building Blocks Organized into Tool Sets** — Individual components are curated into purposeful combinations designed for specific phases and tasks within the SDD cycle.

**Consolidated Knowledge as Foundation** — Your team's existing knowledge (documentation, references, standards, past decisions) becomes the foundation that AI draws from, not just its training data.
