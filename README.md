## üîë Key Concepts

### Specification Driven Development (SDD)

The iterative, specification-first workflow that powers the AI-centric approach:

1. **Interview prompts** gather needs and context
2. A **plan document** is produced and broken into sections or epics
3. Each section is specified through a detailed **Project Requirement Document (PRD)**
4. The PRD drives a well-defined **task list**
5. **Implementation prompts** produce results

This SDD cycle runs continuously across all phases, with Core Principles evaluated at every step.

### The Toolkit Philosophy

**Principles First, Then Tools** ‚Äî Every building block in your toolkit (prompts, skills, MCP servers) should be evaluated against the six Core Principles. If a tool accelerates development but undermines a principle, it doesn't belong.

**Building Blocks Organized into Tool Sets** ‚Äî Individual components are curated into purposeful combinations designed for specific phases and tasks within the SDD cycle.

**Consolidated Knowledge as Foundation** ‚Äî Your team's existing knowledge (documentation, references, standards, past decisions) becomes the foundation that AI draws from, not just its training data.
